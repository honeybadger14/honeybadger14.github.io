Health services

In 1921 the British Red Cross established the first blood transfusion service in the UK. Medical advances meant blood transfusions were increasingly successful but facilities to store blood were unavailable at this time.

Percy Lane Oliver, a member of London Branch of the British Red Cross, recruited donors prepared to give blood, day or night. The British Red Cross continued to provide help with the blood transfusion service in an ancillary specialist role until 1987.
